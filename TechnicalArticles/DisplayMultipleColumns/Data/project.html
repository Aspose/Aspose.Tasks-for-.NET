1. Five to Eight Weeks Before Moving
  2. Planning the Move This section prepares you to plan your move based on your timetable and moving requirements.
    3. Calculate moving expenses Expenses may include supplies, movers, rental truck, and deposits and fees for setting up a new residence.
    4. Determine the best method of moving Moving methods may include hiring moving services, doing it yourself, or combining approaches.
    5. Create a moving-expense receipt file
    6. Create a moving binder Create a binder to store moving plan, important move documentation, and a log of move notes and activities.
  7. Household Administration Household administration includes all activities that are required to transfer a household from location to another aside from moving physical items.
    8. Finances and Insurance Tasks related to finances and insurance include all changes to banking activity, insurance, and costs of the move.
      9. Review household finances
      10. Verify that your belongings are insured for the move Your homeowner or renter's insurance may cover your belongings. Standard insurance coverage from a moving service rarely covers your belongings for replacement value.
      11. Appraise valuables specifically insured for the move
      12. Transfer insurance policies to your new address Instruct your insurance agent to transfer property, fire, and auto insurance.
      13. Transfer medical insurance to your new location Inform your company's benefit representative of your new address. If you're changing jobs, verify that you are receiving interim health insurance.
    14. Notifications Notifications include notifying friends, family, government agencies, magazines you subscribe to, or others who need your new address.
      15. Notify your employer of your moving dates and new address
      16. Transfer, sell, or discontinue any memberships Memberships may include clubs, gyms, organizations, or community groups.
    17. Vital Services and Records Vital services may include medical, schools, and veterinary care.
      18. Organize important records Important records may include prescriptions, optical prescriptions, x-rays, medical and dental records, school records, and birth certificates.
      19. Contact your doctor, dentist, and vet for referrals
      20. Request additional refills of vital medications
    21. Your New Residence Your New Residence includes all activities that will help you get accustomed to your new community or set up your new residence. These activities also include establishing utilities and government services.
      22. Collect shopping and dining guides from your new community
      23. Determine the location of the police, fire department and hospitals
      24. Change drivers/vehicle license address if required
      25. Place orders for new furniture
  26. Moving Moving encompasses all activities involved in physically moving your possessions from one location to another.
    27. Movers Movers refers to all activities done by or in conjunction with a moving service.
      28. Obtain estimates from several moving services On-site estimates tend to be more accurate and save you from surprises when the movers arrive.
      29. Request references from each moving service
      30. Select moving service
      31. Discuss rough details with movers Rough details may include costs, packing requirements, preliminary move dates, and insurance availability.
    32. Packing Packing includes all activities in preparing and packing your possessions to be moved.
      33. Inventory and organize all possessions It is important to decide which possessions will be moved by movers, moved by you, or donated and sold. You should move valuable or irreplaceable items such as legal documents, jewelry, photos, or home videos yourself.
      34. Begin using food and cleaning supplies currently on hand Avoid shopping for anything you'll have to move.
      35. Start cleaning closets, bookcases, and other storage areas
      36. Conduct a sale or donate to charity
      37. Decide what furniture items you want to keep Obtain a floor plan of your new residence if possible.
      38. Reserve storage if necessary
